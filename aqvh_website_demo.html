How Quantum Computers Work

⚛️ Classical vs Quantum Bits

Classical Bit: Only 0 or 1 at any time

Quantum Bit (Qubit): Can be 0, 1, or BOTH simultaneously (Superposition)

This is why quantum computers are exponentially more powerful!

🌀 Superposition Explained

Qubits exist in multiple states simultaneously...

Mathematical representation: |ψ⟩ = α|0⟩ + β|1⟩

This allows quantum computers to explore many solutions in parallel!

📏 Measurement & Collapse

When measured, qubits collapse to either 0 or 1...

The outcome is truly random and probabilistic.

This randomness is what we use for quantum cryptography!

🔧 Hadamard Gate (H)

The gate that creates superposition from a ground state...

Input: |0⟩ (qubit in ground state)

Output: (|0⟩ + |1⟩)/√2 (superposition)

This is the MOST IMPORTANT gate for QRNG!
